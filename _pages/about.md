I am a second-year PhD student in the [School of Computational Science and Engineering](https://cse.gatech.edu/) at Georgia Institute of Technology, advised by Prof. [Elizabeth Qian](https://www.elizabethqian.com/). 

My research focuses on **reduced-order models** and **structure-exploiting methods** for Bayesian inverse problems. I am broadly interested in scalable uncertainty quantification for expensive computational models — particularly likelihood-informed subspace methods, transport maps, and ensemble filtering for high-dimensional inference.

Before Georgia Tech, I completed my MSc at the University of Oxford, supervised by Prof. Geoff Nicholls.

## Research Interests
- Bayesian inverse problems and uncertainty quantification
- Likelihood-informed subspace (LIS) methods
- Ensemble Kalman methods and transport maps
- Dimension reduction for high-dimensional inference
- Scientific machine learning and operator inference
